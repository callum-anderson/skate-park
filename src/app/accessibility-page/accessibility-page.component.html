Accessibility Statement

This website was built to be accessible for all users, something which we consider especially important as we are a community group.
The website has been designed with easy access to information in mind, and to comply with the World Wide Web Consortium's standard and has the following features.

General

The site is built using HTML and cascading style sheets (CSS). This creates a quick loading website that is accessible to screen readers and is usable in most browsers, including older browsers and browsers available on non-Windows systems.

Text

All text within the site is enclosed in the relevant HTML tags to faciliate screen readers. All information is available when switching to text-only mode.

Images/Design

All images and icons have ALT tags. Where appropriate, ALT tags provide screen reader users and those with images switched off with a description of the content of the picture or icon.

Text/sound alternative to video

Unfortunately we do not have the resources at present to provide transcripts/subtitles and audio descriptions for video content. We hope to do so in the future.

Feedback

Should you have any concerns or queries around accessibility to this website, please do get in touch with us.

Details of the various ways to get in touch are included on our contact page.
